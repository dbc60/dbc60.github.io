This section exists because justice work matters to me, even if it isn’t the main focus of this blog. Over the years, I’ve wanted a place to collect what I’m learning about racial justice—especially the work of Black scholars, activists, and creators—and to acknowledge the responsibility I feel to stay engaged, aware, and accountable.

Why This Exists

My technical work is one part of my life, but it doesn’t exist in a vacuum. The world we build software in is shaped by systems that are unequal and often unjust. This section is where I try to understand those systems better and point readers toward people who understand them far more deeply than I do.

Here, I hope to:

  • Lift up Black voices – Not speaking for anyone, but directing attention to people with lived experience
  • Collect meaningful resources – Articles, books, research, organizations, and actions that have taught me something
  • Reflect on what I’m learning – Noticing where my understanding is growing or where I’ve been wrong
  • Stay committed – Treating justice work as an ongoing responsibility, not a momentary statement

What This Covers

The starting point is racial justice in America, rooted in the principles of the Black Lives Matter movement. That’s the foundation of this section. As I continue learning, the scope may widen to include related issues—Indigenous rights, workers’ rights, environmental justice—but always in a way that honors where this work begins.

How I Approach This

I’m approaching this as a learner, not an authority. I try to:

  • Listen first and default to linking to Black scholars and creators
  • Share resources that have lasting value, rather than reactive commentary
  • Revisit and revise my understanding over time
  • Make this section something that grows with me rather than pretending I have it all figured out

A Note About Perspective

I am not Black, and I will make mistakes. I don’t pretend to speak for anyone. If anything here feels incomplete, inaccurate, or misguided, I welcome your feedback at doug.cuthbertson@gmail.com. I want this space to be thoughtful, responsible, and open to correction.

This is an ongoing project. I’ll add to it as I keep learning, unlearning, and finding better ways to engage.
